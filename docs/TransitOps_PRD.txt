TRANSITOPS - PRODUCT REQUIREMENTS DOCUMENT
1. Executive Summary
TransitOps is a centralized transport operations platform that digitizes the full lifecycle of fleet management: vehicle
registry, driver management, dispatching, maintenance, fuel and expense tracking, and analytics, while enforcing
hard business rules that prevent unsafe or invalid dispatch decisions. This document extends the baseline problem
statement with a set of advanced, real-world differentiator modules that go beyond CRUD-and-workflow coverage:
computed driver risk scoring, dispatch recommendation intelligence, predictive maintenance, duty-hour compliance,
cost leakage detection, a compliance document vault, and a natural-language operations assistant.
The objective is twofold: (1) fully satisfy every mandatory requirement and business rule in the official problem
statement, and (2) demonstrate genuine operational intelligence, the kind of system a real logistics company would
actually want to run on, so the submission is differentiated from every other team solving the identical brief.
Aspect Summary
Problem space Manual, spreadsheet-driven transport operations: scheduling conflicts, missed maintenance,
expired licenses, inaccurate costing, no real-time visibility.
Core users Fleet Manager, Dispatcher/Driver, Safety Officer, Financial Analyst
Baseline scope Auth + RBAC, Dashboard/KPIs, Vehicle Registry, Driver Management, Trip Management,
Maintenance, Fuel & Expense, Reports & Analytics
Differentiators Computed Safety Score engine, Dispatch Recommendation Engine, Predictive
Maintenance, Duty-Hour Compliance, Cost Leakage Detection, Compliance Document
Vault, NL Ops Assistant, Live Ops Map, Customer Tracking Link
Delivery format Responsive web app; role-based dashboards; real-time status updates
2. Problem Statement and Goals
2.1 Background
Many logistics companies still run operations on spreadsheets and paper logbooks. This creates scheduling
conflicts, underutilized vehicles, missed maintenance windows, expired driver licenses, inaccurate expense tracking,
and near-zero real-time operational visibility for decision-makers.
2.2 Product Goals
(cid:127) Digitize the complete transport operations lifecycle in a single system of record.
(cid:127) Enforce hard business rules automatically so invalid states (double-booked drivers, overloaded vehicles, expired
licenses on active trips) are structurally impossible, not just discouraged.
(cid:127) Give every role a real-time, relevant operational view instead of a single generic dashboard.
(cid:127) Move from reactive operations (fix problems after they happen) to predictive operations (surface risk before it
becomes a failure) using the operational data the platform already collects.
(cid:127) Provide a conversational interface so non-technical managers can query fleet state in plain language instead of
navigating multiple screens.
2.3 Non-Goals (Out of Scope)
(cid:127) Payroll processing or HR management for drivers/staff.
(cid:127) Native mobile apps (a mobile-first responsive web experience is in scope; native iOS/Android apps are not).
(cid:127) Hardware/IoT integration for live GPS telemetry (simulated/mock telemetry is used for scoring and risk features;
real GPS hardware integration is a future-phase item).
(cid:127) Accounting/invoicing/GST compliance - expense tracking feeds analytics only, not a ledger.

4. Baseline Feature Scope (Problem-Statement Mandatory)
These modules satisfy every requirement and mandatory deliverable in the official TransitOps problem statement.
They are foundational; the differentiator layer in Section 5 is built on top of this data.
Module Coverage
Authentication & RBAC Email/password login, session handling, role-based access control gating every screen
and API route by role.
Dashboard KPIs: Active Vehicles, Available Vehicles, Vehicles in Maintenance, Active Trips,
Pending Trips, Drivers On Duty, Fleet Utilization (%). Filterable by vehicle type, status,
region.
Vehicle Registry Master vehicle list: unique Registration Number, Model, Type, Max Load Capacity,
Odometer, Acquisition Cost, Status (Available / On Trip / In Shop / Retired).
Driver Management Driver profiles: Name, License Number/Category/Expiry, Contact, Safety Score, Status
(Available / On Trip / Off Duty / Suspended).
Trip Management Create trips with source, destination, vehicle, driver, cargo weight, planned distance.
Lifecycle: Draft to Dispatched to Completed, or Cancelled.
Maintenance Maintenance records per vehicle; creating an active record auto-flips vehicle status to In
Shop and removes it from the dispatch pool.
Fuel & Expense Management Fuel logs (liters, cost, date) and other expenses (tolls, maintenance); automatic total
operational cost (Fuel + Maintenance) per vehicle.
Reports & Analytics Fuel Efficiency (Distance/Fuel), Fleet Utilization, Operational Cost, Vehicle ROI =
(Revenue - (Maintenance + Fuel)) / Acquisition Cost. CSV export mandatory, PDF
optional.
4.1 Mandatory Business Rules (Hard Constraints)
(cid:127) Vehicle registration number must be unique.
(cid:127) Retired or In Shop vehicles must never appear in dispatch selection.
(cid:127) Drivers with expired licenses or Suspended status cannot be assigned to trips.
(cid:127) A driver or vehicle already marked On Trip cannot be assigned to another trip.
(cid:127) Cargo Weight must not exceed the vehicle's maximum load capacity.
(cid:127) Dispatching a trip automatically sets both vehicle and driver status to On Trip.
(cid:127) Completing a trip automatically reverts both vehicle and driver status to Available.
(cid:127) Cancelling a dispatched trip restores vehicle and driver to Available.
(cid:127) Creating an active maintenance record automatically sets vehicle status to In Shop.
(cid:127) Closing maintenance restores vehicle to Available (unless the vehicle is Retired).

5. Advanced Differentiator Features
Every team at this hackathon receives the identical problem statement, so baseline coverage alone cannot
differentiate a submission. The features below are deliberately not required by the PS but reflect real operational
needs of transport/logistics businesses, and are engineered to reuse the same underlying data (trips, fuel logs,
maintenance records) the baseline system already captures, maximizing impact per hour of build time.
5.1 Computed Driver Risk & Safety Score Engine [P0 - Core Differentiator | Effort: Medium]
What it is: Replaces the PS's static, manually-entered Safety Score field with a continuously computed score
derived from real operational signals: harsh-event flags, on-time delivery rate, fuel-anomaly association,
cancelled/incident trip history, and duty-hour violations.
Why it wins: A manually-typed number is trivial to fake and demonstrates no engineering depth. A computed,
auditable, tamper-resistant score is exactly what a real Safety Officer needs and what almost no other team will
attempt in an 8-hour build.
Key requirements:
(cid:127) Weighted formula: On-Time Rate (35%) + Incident-Free Rate (30%) + Duty-Hour Compliance (20%) +
Fuel-Anomaly-Free Rate (15%), normalized to a 0-100 scale.
(cid:127) Score recalculates automatically after every completed trip, incident log, or duty-hour flag.
(cid:127) Full score history/audit trail per driver, viewable by Safety Officer and Fleet Manager.
(cid:127) Configurable weighting via Settings, so Safety Officer can tune emphasis per organization policy.
5.2 Dispatch Recommendation Engine [P0 - Core Differentiator | Effort: Medium]
What it is: Instead of a raw dropdown of available drivers and vehicles, the system ranks the best driver-vehicle
pairing for a given trip using a scoring model that weighs safety score, vehicle proximity/idle time, cargo-capacity fit,
and fleet-wear balancing.
Why it wins: This is the single highest-leverage feature for a live demo: it visibly makes a smart decision in real time
instead of just validating a manual choice, and it directly answers the real-world question dispatchers actually
struggle with: who/what should I assign, not just is this assignment valid.
Key requirements:
(cid:127) Ranks all eligible (rule-valid) driver+vehicle combinations by a composite fitness score.
(cid:127) Cargo-capacity fit: penalizes large capacity mismatch (assigning a 2-ton truck to a 50kg parcel) to encourage
efficient utilization.
(cid:127) Fleet-wear balancing: deprioritizes vehicles/drivers with disproportionately high recent utilization to spread wear
evenly.
(cid:127) Dispatcher can override the recommendation; overrides are logged for later analysis.

5.3 Predictive Maintenance Alerts [P0 - Core Differentiator | Effort: Medium]
What it is: Extends the reactive maintenance module into a predictive one: the system forecasts upcoming
maintenance needs from odometer trend velocity and historical maintenance interval patterns per vehicle, rather
than waiting for someone to manually raise a request after something breaks.
Why it wins: The PS's maintenance module is purely reactive. Predicting the need before failure is the difference
between a logbook digitization and an actual operations platform, a distinction judges evaluating real-world
applicability will notice.
Key requirements:
(cid:127) Tracks odometer-reading velocity (km/day) per vehicle from trip completions.
(cid:127) Compares current odometer against the vehicle's historical maintenance interval (e.g., service every 10,000km) to
forecast a predicted service due date.
(cid:127) Auto-generates a Maintenance Due Soon alert on the Dashboard and Notifications when a vehicle is within a
configurable threshold (e.g., 500km or 7 days) of its predicted due point.
(cid:127) Distinguishes forecast-driven alerts visually from reactive fault-reported maintenance records.
5.4 Duty-Hour & Fatigue Compliance Tracking [P1 - High Value | Effort: Low-Medium]
What it is: Tracks each driver's consecutive driving hours and trips per rolling 24-hour window, flagging drivers who
approach or breach safe duty-hour limits, in line with real-world hours-of-service norms for commercial transport.
Why it wins: This is a genuine regulatory/safety concern in real fleet operations that the PS never mentions at all.
Building it signals domain understanding beyond implementing the PDF, a strong signal to judges with any
operations/logistics background.
Key requirements:
(cid:127) Rolling-window duty-hour calculation per driver from trip start/end timestamps.
(cid:127) Configurable maximum duty-hour threshold (default 10 hours/24h window, adjustable in Settings).
(cid:127) Drivers approaching the threshold are flagged amber; drivers who breach it are blocked from new dispatch until
reset, feeding the same eligibility check used in Section 5.2.
(cid:127) Fatigue-risk indicator surfaced on the Safety Officer dashboard and Dispatch Recommendation Engine.
5.5 Cost Leakage & Anomaly Detection [P1 - High Value | Effort: Low]
What it is: Reuses the same statistical-baseline approach as driver risk scoring, applied to cost: flags a vehicle
whose cost-per-km has spiked significantly against its own historical baseline or against similar vehicles in its class,
surfacing possible fuel pilferage, overbilled maintenance, or inefficient routing.
Why it wins: Financial Analysts in real fleets spend significant time manually hunting for exactly this kind of leakage
in spreadsheets. Automating the detection is a low-effort, high-perceived-value feature since it reuses fuel/expense
data the baseline system already stores.
Key requirements:
(cid:127) Computes rolling average cost-per-km per vehicle and flags deviations beyond a configurable standard-deviation
threshold.
(cid:127) Cross-vehicle comparison within the same vehicle Type/class to catch outliers even without individual history.
(cid:127) Anomalies surfaced in Reports & Analytics and pushed to the Notification system for the Financial Analyst role.
(cid:127) Each anomaly links directly to the underlying fuel log / maintenance record for one-click investigation.

5.6 Unified Compliance Document Vault [P1 - High Value | Effort: Low]
What it is: Extends the PS's single license-expiry concept into a full compliance calendar covering every document
a real commercial vehicle needs: insurance, pollution certificate (PUC), fitness certificate, and permit renewal, each
with expiry tracking and tiered reminders.
Why it wins: The PS only asks for license expiry reminders as a bonus feature; real fleets track 4-5 recurring
compliance documents per vehicle/driver. This closes a real operational gap cheaply and signals attention to
real-world completeness.
Key requirements:
(cid:127) Document model per vehicle/driver: type, document number, issue date, expiry date, uploaded file reference.
(cid:127) Tiered reminders (30 days / 7 days / expired) feeding the Notification system.
(cid:127) Vehicles/drivers with any expired mandatory document are automatically excluded from dispatch eligibility, reusing
the same rule engine as license/status checks.
(cid:127) Single compliance calendar view for the Safety Officer showing all upcoming expiries across the fleet.
5.7 Natural-Language Operations Assistant [P0 - Core Differentiator | Effort: Medium-High]
What it is: A conversational query interface where a Fleet Manager or Financial Analyst can type plain-language
questions such as which vehicles need maintenance this week, show drivers with expiring licenses, or what was our
fuel cost last month, and receive an answer generated live from current platform data.
Why it wins: This is the standout live-demo moment: it is visually memorable, interactive, and directly differentiates
the team's AI/engineering capability from every other team's static dashboards. It also has the highest
wow-factor-per-second of any feature in this document.
Key requirements:
(cid:127) Natural-language input mapped to a constrained set of pre-defined, safe data queries (no arbitrary code execution
against the database).
(cid:127) Role-aware answers: a Driver asking the assistant only sees their own trips/data; a Fleet Manager sees fleet-wide
answers.
(cid:127) Answers rendered as a short natural-language summary plus a supporting table/chart where relevant.
(cid:127) Assistant surfaces a graceful fallback for out-of-scope questions rather than hallucinating.
5.8 Live Fleet Operations Map [P2 - Nice to Have | Effort: Medium]
What it is: A real-time visual map for the Fleet Manager showing all On Trip vehicles with live/simulated position
updates, distinguishing normal trips from those flagged by route-deviation or fatigue alerts.
Why it wins: A live, moving map is an immediately compelling visual centerpiece in any demo and very few teams
will have real-time visualization working cleanly within the time constraints, a strong return on a moderate amount of
build effort.
Key requirements:
(cid:127) Simulated/mock GPS position feed per active trip (interpolated between source and destination) if hardware
telemetry is unavailable.
(cid:127) Real-time push of position + status updates to the Manager dashboard (WebSocket-based).
(cid:127) Visual distinction for at-risk trips (fatigue flag, cost anomaly, route deviation) directly on the map.

5.9 Customer-Facing Shipment Tracking Link [P2 - Nice to Have | Effort: Low]
What it is: A shareable, read-only, no-login link per trip that an external customer/client can open to see live
shipment status and ETA, the same pattern used by real logistics platforms.
Why it wins: The PS is entirely internal-facing; this feature demonstrates awareness of the actual business model
behind transport operations (the end customer cares about their shipment, not the internal dashboard), for very low
additional engineering cost.
Key requirements:
(cid:127) Unique, unguessable tokenized URL generated per trip at dispatch time.
(cid:127) Read-only view: status, ETA, and origin/destination; no sensitive internal data (costs, driver personal info) exposed.
(cid:127) Link automatically expires/deactivates once the trip is Completed or Cancelled.

7. Non-Functional Requirements
Category Requirement
Performance Dashboard KPIs and dispatch recommendations must render in under 1.5s for a fleet of up to
500 vehicles.
Reliability All status-transition business rules (Section 4.1) must be enforced server-side, never trusted
from client input alone.
Security RBAC enforced at API layer; JWT-based auth; passwords hashed (bcrypt or equivalent); public
tracking links use unguessable tokens with no PII exposure.
Auditability Every automatic status change, score recalculation, and NL assistant query is logged with
actor, timestamp, and before/after state.
Responsiveness Fully responsive layout across desktop and mobile viewports for all core screens.
Extensibility Scoring weights (Safety Score, Cost Anomaly threshold, Duty-Hour limit) configurable via
Settings, not hardcoded.
